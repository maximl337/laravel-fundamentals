                                                                                                                                            miocene James Gray II • 2 months ago

                                                                                                                                            Not a good solution - you're placing your presentation logic in the view. Much better to use the following:

                                                                                                                                            1) Tell Laravel that published_at is a date you want parsed with Carbon: simply place the attribute name in a protected $dates array in your model; e.g. protected $dates = ['published_at'];. That's it! now $article->published_at is automatically a Carbon object.

                                                                                                                                            2) You can further define how you want your dates presented by using accessors. Here you just place a method in your model called getFooAttribute($value) where Foo is your attribute name. In the method you can define how you want your attribute formatted when it's referred to. E.g.

                                                                                                                                            public function getPublishedAtAttribute($value)
{
   return $value->format('Y-m-d'); //Assumes published_at is a Carbon instance
}

                                                                                                                                            This way none of this logic clutters your views and it takes effect everywhere rather than just individual views.

                                                                                                                                            *code untested
